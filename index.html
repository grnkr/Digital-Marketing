3-Hour Marketing Workshop

Master marketing essentials through theory and hands-on exercises

Theory (1.5 Hours)

1. Introduction to Marketing (15 mins)

Definition: Marketing is the process of identifying, anticipating, and satisfying customer needs profitably.

Types:

  • Traditional: TV, radio, print ads, billboards, events
  • Digital: Social media, SEO, email, websites, influencer marketing

The 4Ps of Marketing:

  • Product: What you sell (features, design, quality, branding)
  • Price: What the customer pays (strategy, discounts, offers)
  • Place: Where/how it’s delivered (stores, online, channels)
  • Promotion: How you communicate (advertising, PR, sales, social media)

Importance: Creates awareness, increases sales, builds loyalty, drives growth.

2. Understanding Target Market & Segmentation (15 mins)

Target Market: A specific group of consumers you aim to reach.

Segmentation Types:

  • Demographic: Age, gender, income, education, occupation
  • Geographic: Region, country, climate, city size
  • Psychographic: Lifestyle, personality, values, interests
  • Behavioral: Buying behavior, usage rate, loyalty, benefits sought

Buyer Personas: Fictional profiles of ideal customers.

Example: Techie Tom, 22, gamer, uses Instagram/YouTube, waits for sales.

3. Marketing Strategies (20 mins)

Inbound Marketing: Attracts customers via content (blogging, SEO, social media).

Outbound Marketing: Pushes messages (TV/radio ads, cold calling, email blasts).

Other Strategies:

  • Content Marketing: Blogs, videos, infographics, podcasts
  • Influencer Marketing: Collaborating with popular personalities
  • Social Media Marketing: Engaging on Instagram, X, LinkedIn
  • SEO: Organic traffic via search engines
  • SEM: Paid traffic via Google Ads, Bing Ads

4. Marketing Funnel & Customer Journey (20 mins)

Funnel Stages: Awareness → Interest → Decision → Action.

AIDA Model:

  • Attention: Capture interest (ads, viral content)
  • Interest: Engage (blogs, demos)
  • Desire: Show value (reviews, offers)
  • Action: Convert (buy now, checkout)

Customer Journey: Awareness, Consideration, Purchase, Retention, Advocacy.

5. Branding & Positioning (10 mins)

Branding: Creating a unique identity (name, logo, values).

Positioning: Placing your brand in customers’ minds vs. competitors.

USP: Unique Selling Proposition (what makes you different).

Positioning Strategies: Price-based, quality-based, usage-based, user-based, competitor-based.

6. Analytics and Metrics (10 mins)

Metrics:

  • ROI: Profit vs. marketing spend
  • Conversion Rate: % of users taking action
  • Click-Through Rate (CTR): % clicking ads/links
  • Bounce Rate: % leaving site without interaction

Importance: Data-driven marketing optimizes campaigns.

Tools: Google Analytics, Meta Business Suite, HubSpot.

Practical (1.5 Hours)

1. Create a Marketing Persona (15 mins)

Choose a product/service and define its target audience.

Task: Create a persona with demographics (age, gender, location) and psychographics (interests, values).

Example: Riya Sharma, 24, MCA student, goals: IT job, uses YouTube/LinkedIn.

2. Design a Mini Marketing Plan (30 mins)

Steps:

  • Pick a product/brand
  • Define SMART goals (e.g., 500 app downloads)
  • Choose channels (Instagram, YouTube, email)
  • Outline 4Ps: Product, Price, Place, Promotion

Example: Meal delivery app for students, promoted via Instagram ads and campus stalls.

3. Social Media Campaign Simulation (20 mins)

Task: Create 2–3 posts for a campaign using Canva/Figma.

Include: Hashtags, captions, target platform (e.g., Instagram, X).

Scenarios: College fest, food startup, EdTech app, or social cause.

4. Analytics & Insights (15 mins)

Task: Analyze mockup reports (Google Analytics, Instagram Insights).

Example: Instagram campaign with 12,000 reach, 110 likes, 4 comments (weak engagement).

Interpret metrics like CTR, bounce rate, conversion rate.

5. Quick Group Presentation (10 mins)

Task: Present your campaign idea and marketing plan.

Receive peer feedback and wrap up.
